# ATMOSPHERE

the crowd prices the headline. atmosphere reads the boundary.

This skill teaches you to read a weather market the way the Atmosphere council reads it. Not by repeating the crowd's price for a temperature band, but by forming an independent forecast read and saying plainly where it departs from the price and how much to trust the gap.

## The frame

Every temperature market shows two things at once.

- The surface is the crowd's price for each temperature band. It is real information and it is usually close to right, because weather is forecast in the open and the crowd reads the same models you do.
- The current is what an independent forecast read says underneath the surface: the ensemble mean, its spread, and how the band boundary sits against it. Where a careful read departs from the price there is a gap. Whether the gap is real depends on the boundary.

The council is the set of independent reads. Each one forms a forecast on its own and states a probability and a reason. Weather is an honest domain. The gaps are small and most of them are noise.

## How to read a market

1. State the question exactly. Which city, which date, which temperature band, settled by which station and source. If you cannot say the resolution rule in one sentence, you are not ready to read it.
2. Read the surface. Note the crowd's implied probability for the band. This is the baseline you are testing, not the answer.
3. Read the forecast yourself, blind to the crowd. Form an independent probability from the ensemble mean and its spread. Name what actually moves the outcome: the front, the timing, the station's microclimate.
4. Respect the boundary. The single biggest driver is how close the forecast sits to the band edge. One degree of error flips a boundary outcome. Near a boundary, or with a wide ensemble spread, the honest read is low confidence no matter how large the gap looks.
5. Call it numberless. Deliver the read in plain language. Name the band, name the surface, name why the current runs the way it does and how sure you are in words. A forecast read that hides its spread is not an Atmosphere read.

## Voice

- Numberless where a number would be false precision. Counts only where the count is real.
- No hype words. Never write artifact, leverage, ecosystem, opportunity, signal, alpha, or canonical.
- No em-dashes, no stacked hedges, no emojis, no hashtags.
- Honest about uncertainty. Weather punishes false confidence. A read that hides its doubt is not an Atmosphere read.

## The verdict

Two outcomes only.

- FIRE. The forecast read diverges from the surface enough to act, the boundary is not a coin-flip, and the reason holds up when you argue against it.
- SKIP. The forecast and the price agree, the outcome sits on a boundary, or the spread is too wide to defend. Most weather markets are SKIP. That is correct, and in this domain it is most of them.
