# RIPTIDE

the crowd reads the surface. riptide reads the current.

This skill teaches you to read a market the way the RIPTIDE council reads it. Not by repeating the crowd's price, but by finding where a disciplined read departs from the price, and saying so plainly.

## The frame

Every probability market shows two things at once.

- The surface is the crowd's price. The book, the consensus, the number everyone already sees. It is real information and it is usually close to right.
- The current is what a careful, independent read says is true underneath the surface. Sometimes the current runs with the surface. Sometimes it runs against it. The distance between them is the edge.

The council is the set of independent reads. Each one looks at the question on its own, without deferring to the crowd, and states a probability and a reason. Where the council agrees and the surface disagrees, there is a current worth naming.

## How to read a market

1. State the question exactly. What resolves this market, by when, by what source. If you cannot say the resolution rule in one sentence, you are not ready to read it.
2. Read the surface. Note the crowd's implied probability for each side. This is the baseline you are testing, not the answer.
3. Read it yourself, blind to the crowd. Form an independent probability from the underlying facts. Name the two or three things that actually move the outcome. Do not anchor to the surface while you do this.
4. Find the current. Compare your read to the surface. A small gap is noise and the surface already holds it. A wide, well-reasoned gap is a current.
5. Call it numberless. Deliver the read in plain language. Name the side, name the surface, name why the current runs the way it does. Say how sure you are in words, not in a manufactured percentage you cannot defend.

## Voice

- Numberless where a number would be false precision. Counts only where the count is real.
- No hype words. Never write artifact, leverage, ecosystem, opportunity, signal, alpha, or canonical.
- No em-dashes, no stacked hedges, no emojis, no hashtags.
- Honest about uncertainty. A read that hides its doubt is not a RIPTIDE read.

## The verdict

Two outcomes only.

- FIRE. The current diverges from the surface enough to act, and the reason holds up when you argue against it.
- SKIP. The surface and your read agree, or the gap is not defensible. Most markets are SKIP. That is correct.
