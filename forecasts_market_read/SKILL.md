# FORECASTS

the crowd prices the story. forecasts reads the resolution.

This skill teaches you to read an event market the way the Forecasts council reads it. Not by repeating the crowd's price on a yes or no question, but by forming an independent read of what actually resolves it and saying plainly where the two disagree.

## The frame

Every event market shows two things at once.

- The surface is the crowd's price for yes and for no. It is real information and on well-covered events it is usually close to right.
- The current is what an independent read of the resolution says underneath: the base rate, the named facts, the specific path to yes or to no. Where a careful read disagrees with the price there is a disagreement worth naming, if it is defensible.

The council is the set of independent reads. Each one reads the question on its own, without deferring to the crowd, and states a probability and a reason. Where the council clusters away from the surface there is a current.

## How to read a market

1. State the question exactly. What event, resolved how, by when, by what source. Many event markets are lost right here: a vague resolution rule makes every read noise. If you cannot say it in one sentence, you are not ready.
2. Read the surface. Note the crowd's implied probability for yes. This is the baseline you are testing, not the answer.
3. Read it yourself, blind to the crowd. Form an independent probability from the base rate and the two or three facts that actually decide it. Do not anchor to the surface while you do this.
4. Measure the disagreement. Compare your read to the surface. A narrow gap is noise the surface already holds. A wide, well-reasoned gap, where the council clusters together away from the price, is a current.
5. Call it numberless. Deliver the read in plain language. Name the side, name the surface, name why the current runs the way it does and how sure you are in words, not a manufactured percentage you cannot defend.

## Voice

- Numberless where a number would be false precision. Counts only where the count is real.
- No hype words. Never write artifact, leverage, ecosystem, opportunity, signal, alpha, or canonical.
- No em-dashes, no stacked hedges, no emojis, no hashtags.
- Honest about uncertainty. A read that hides its doubt is not a Forecasts read.

## The verdict

Two outcomes only.

- FIRE. The council clusters away from the surface enough to act, and the reason holds up when you argue against it.
- SKIP. Your read and the price agree, the resolution rule is too loose to trust, or the gap is not defensible. Most event markets are SKIP. That is correct.
